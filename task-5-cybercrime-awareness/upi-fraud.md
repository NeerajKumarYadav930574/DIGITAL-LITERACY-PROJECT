# Case Study: UPI "Request Money" Fraud
**Category:** Online Payment Fraud

## 1. What the Crime Is
UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a form of digital financial crime that uses social engineering to trick victims into authorizing unauthorized transfers. Unlike a technical hack, the criminal manipulates the victim into voluntarily giving up their money by exploiting their lack of technical knowledge regarding how UPI "Collect Requests" work.

## 2. How it Happens (Step-by-Step)
1.  **The Hook:** The fraudster poses as a buyer on a platform like OLX or Facebook Marketplace and expresses urgent interest in an item the victim is selling.
2.  **The Deception:** The attacker claims they are sending a "payment" or "token amount" to the victim's UPI ID.
3.  **The "Request":** Instead of sending money, the attacker uses the "Request Money" feature in a UPI app. The victim receives a notification to "Pay" or "Approve."
4.  **The Trap:** The attacker stays on the phone with the victim, rushing them and saying, "Just click 'Pay' and enter your PIN to receive the money in your bank."
5.  **The Theft:** The victim enters their PIN. Since a PIN is only required to *send* money, the funds are instantly deducted from the victim's account and sent to the attacker.

## 3. Target Audience
The primary targets are **students, elderly citizens, and small-scale online sellers** who may be comfortable using digital apps but are unaware that receiving money *never* requires entering a UPI PIN.

## 4. Consequences
The immediate consequence is the **permanent loss of funds**, as UPI transactions are instantaneous and difficult to reverse. Secondary consequences include significant **psychological stress**, a sense of violation, and a long-term distrust of digital banking systems.

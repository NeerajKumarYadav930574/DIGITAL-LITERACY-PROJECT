# Social Media Responsibility Checklist
### Student Digital Ambassador Guide

This checklist provides essential guidelines for maintaining a professional and safe digital presence as a college student.

| Category | Do's (Best Practices) | Don'ts (Avoid These) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Privacy** | Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) and audit your privacy settings regularly. | Don't post sensitive info like your home address, phone number, or real-time location. |
| **Professionalism** | Use a professional profile picture and curate your "About" section to reflect your skills. | Don't post content showing illegal activities or unprofessional behavior that could alert recruiters. |
| **Credibility** | Fact-check news and articles from multiple reliable sources before sharing. | Don't spread unverified rumors, "leaked" exam papers, or misinformation. |
| **Digital Wellness** | Set daily screen time limits to ensure social media doesn't interfere with your studies. | Don't compare your real life to the "highlight reels" of others, which can impact mental health. |
| **Etiquette** | Use your platform to highlight academic projects, certifications, and volunteer work. | Don't engage in cyberbullying, trolling, or posting derogatory comments about peers or faculty. |
